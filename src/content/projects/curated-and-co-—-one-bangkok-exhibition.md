For the One Bangkok pop-up, we treated the retail footprint as a small-scale exhibition rather than a standard shop. The goal was to give each pair of shoes its own "moment," moving away from crowded shelving in favor of clean pedestals and focused accent lighting.

The design uses a neutral, architectural palette to match the premium feel of the development while ensuring the product remains the centerpiece. We focused on open circulation and a stripped-back aesthetic, allowing the craftsmanship of the collection to be viewed like art in a gallery. It’s a quiet, elevated space that prioritizes the experience of discovery over traditional sales-floor clutter.
